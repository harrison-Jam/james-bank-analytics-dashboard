# 🏦 JAMES BANK ANALYTICS DASHBOARD

## 📊 Project Overview

This project is a **comprehensive Excel-based banking analytics solution** designed to simulate real-world financial reporting for a retail banking institution.

It consists of **4 interactive dashboards** built entirely in Microsoft Excel using:

- Power Pivot (Data Model)
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Pivot Tables & Charts
- Slicers for interactivity
- Professional dashboard UI design

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

The dashboards are designed to answer key business questions:

- How is the bank performing financially?
- What is the level of credit risk (NPA)?
- Are customers adopting digital channels?
- Which customer segments drive revenue and growth?

---

## 📁 Dashboards Included

### 1️⃣ Executive Overview
Provides a high-level summary for management:
- Total Transaction Amount
- Loan Portfolio
- NPA %
- Total Customers
- Fee Income
- Regional performance

---

### 2️⃣ Credit Risk & Loans
Focuses on loan portfolio health and risk exposure:
- Total Loan Book & Outstanding Balance
- NPA Amount & NPA %
- Loan Type Risk Analysis
- Loan Status Distribution
- Top High Exposure Customers

---

### 3️⃣ Transactions & Digital Performance
Analyzes transaction efficiency and digital adoption:
- Total Transactions
- Failed Transaction %
- Digital Channel %
- Average Transaction Value
- Transactions by Channel
- Monthly Transaction Trends

---

### 4️⃣ Customer Analytics
Provides insights into customer demographics and segmentation:
- Total Customers
- Average Customer Income
- HNI Contribution %
- Customer Growth YoY
- Income Distribution
- Age Distribution
- Segment Contribution %

---

## 🧠 Key Insights

- Retail customers represent the largest portion of the customer base  
- Digital channels account for a significant share of total transactions  
- ATM transactions show higher failure rates compared to digital channels  
- Business and personal loans contribute significantly to NPA levels  
- High-income customers contribute disproportionately to transaction value
